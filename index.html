The Mechanics of My World

When I was in second grade, my uncle brought home a DIY solar system kit. The models were fun, but it was the small booklet at the bottom of the box that changed everything. It was my first realization that there is a massive, complex system governing the universe, and we can actually understand it.

That drive to deconstruct things became my greatest survival tool. Moving schools seven times—including a massive jump from Canada to Bangalore at thirteen—taught me to approach new environments exactly like science: observe, look for patterns, and figure out the mechanics.

Growing up in a Marwari family, business talk and "what problem does this solve?" was normal dinner conversation. Today, my two sides meet right there: the scientific curiosity to obsess over how the universe works, and the ingrained, practical sense to build things that actually fix problems.
